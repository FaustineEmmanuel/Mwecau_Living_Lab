Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our conservation efforts

Mwecau Living Lab is a conservation initiative focused on protecting endangered plant species in the Western Usambara Mountains of Tanzania. It's a collaborative project between Mwenge Catholic University (MWECAU) and KU Leuven, working to combine scientific research, community engagement, and sustainable conservation practices.

There are several ways to get involved! You can participate in our workshops and training sessions, volunteer for field activities, donate to support specific conservation projects, or partner with us for research and community programs. Contact us through our form above for specific opportunities.

We currently focus on six critically endangered plant species native to the Western Usambara Mountains: Allanblackia stuhlmannii, Margaritaria discoidea, Ocotea usambarensis, Greenwayodendron suaveolens, Cephalosphaera usambarensis, and Prunus africana. Each has significant ecological and cultural importance to the region.
