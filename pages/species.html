Endangered Plant Species

Our living lab focuses on the conservation of six endangered plant species in the Western Usambara Mountains that are being affected by climate change. These species are not only ecologically important but also have cultural and economic significance for local communities.

Margaritaria discoidea

Local Name: Mshamana

Family: Phyllanthaceae

Conservation Status: Endangered

Survival Rate: 64%

Description: A deciduous tree growing up to 30m tall with medicinal properties. Its bark is used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments.

Threats: Habitat loss, climate change, and over-harvesting for medicinal use.

Conservation Actions: Propagation in nurseries, community education, and sustainable harvesting practices.

Musa sapientum

Local Name: Banana

Family: Musaceae

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Survival Rate: 78%

Description: A herbaceous plant with large leaf blades. Traditional banana varieties in the region face challenges from changing climate patterns.

Threats: Climate change, pests, diseases, and competition from commercial varieties.

Conservation Actions: Seed banking, cultivation of traditional varieties, and research on climate-resilient strains.

Coffea arabica

Local Name: Coffee

Family: Rubiaceae

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Survival Rate: 71%

Description: A shrub or small tree native to the Ethiopian highlands but widely cultivated in Tanzania. Local varieties have adapted to specific conditions in the Usambara Mountains.

Threats: Climate change, coffee leaf rust, and competition from commercial varieties.

Conservation Actions: Preserving local coffee varieties, sustainable farming practices, and community involvement.

Rauvolfia caffra

Local Name: Msesewe

Family: Apocynaceae

Conservation Status: Endangered

Survival Rate: 52%

Description: A tropical evergreen tree that can grow up to 40m tall. It has important medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine.

Threats: Deforestation, over-harvesting for medicinal use, and climate change.

Conservation Actions: Protected cultivation, sustainable harvesting guidelines, and community education.

Albizia schimperiana

Local Name: Mruka

Family: Fabaceae

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Survival Rate: 68%

Description: A deciduous tree that can grow up to 35m tall. It is nitrogen-fixing and has ecological importance for soil improvement.

Threats: Habitat loss due to agricultural expansion, logging, and climate change.

Conservation Actions: Reforestation programs, community-based conservation, and sustainable land use practices.

Bridelia micrantha

Local Name: Mmarie

Family: Phyllanthaceae

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Survival Rate: 65%

Description: A semi-deciduous tree growing up to 20m tall with medicinal properties. The bark, roots, and leaves are used in traditional medicine.

Threats: Habitat degradation, over-harvesting for medicinal use, and climate change.

Conservation Actions: Sustainable harvesting practices, cultivation in botanical gardens, and community education.

Our Conservation Approach

Research

Studying the ecology, genetics, and climate adaptability of endangered plant species.

Community Engagement

Working with local communities to develop sustainable conservation practices.

Preservation

Implementing in-situ and ex-situ conservation methods to protect endangered species.
