Our Mission

The Mwecau Living Lab aims to find common ground between conservation efforts and community livelihoods in the Western Usambara Mountains, Tanzania.

Our mission is to raise awareness about the conservation of six endangered plant species affected by climate change, share our goals and activities, provide access to research findings, facilitate collaboration between institutions and communities, and showcase visual documentation of our activities.

Through research, education, and community engagement, we work to establish sustainable practices that benefit both the environment and local communities.

Our Vision

We envision a future where the rich biodiversity of the Western Usambara Mountains is preserved for generations to come, and where local communities thrive through sustainable practices and environmental stewardship.

By linking conservation to community livelihoods, we aim to create a model of sustainable development that can be replicated in other regions facing similar environmental challenges.

Project Theme

"Finding Common Ground: Living Labs for Linking Conservation to Community Livelihood in the Western Usambara Mountains, Tanzania"

The Western Usambara Mountains in Tanzania are a biodiversity hotspot facing significant challenges due to climate change. Our living lab approach creates a space for collaborative research, community engagement, and practical conservation efforts.

By focusing on six endangered plant species that are culturally and economically important to local communities, we aim to develop conservation strategies that also support sustainable livelihoods.
