VLIR-UOS Collaborative Project

The Mwecau Living Lab is a collaborative project between Mwenge Catholic University (MWECAU) in Tanzania and Catholic University Leuven (KU Leuven) in Belgium, funded by the VLIR-UOS program.

This international partnership brings together expertise in conservation, environmental management, and community development to address the challenges of biodiversity preservation in the Western Usambara Mountains.

Mwenge Catholic University (MWECAU)

Mwenge Catholic University is a higher learning institution located in Moshi, Tanzania. Founded in 2005, MWECAU offers a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs with a focus on quality education and service to the community.

In the Mwecau Living Lab project, MWECAU provides local expertise, facilitates community engagement, and coordinates field activities in the Western Usambara Mountains.

The university's Department of Environmental Studies brings valuable knowledge of local ecology, biodiversity, and conservation challenges specific to the region.

Catholic University Leuven (KU Leuven)

KU Leuven is one of Europe's oldest and most renowned research universities, founded in 1425 in Leuven, Belgium. The university is known for its strong research programs in various fields, including environmental science and sustainability.

In the Mwecau Living Lab project, KU Leuven contributes research expertise, methodological guidance, and international perspectives on conservation and climate change adaptation.

The university's faculty brings experience in biodiversity conservation, climate science, and sustainable development to support the project's goals.

About VLIR-UOS

VLIR-UOS supports partnerships between universities and university colleges in Flanders, Belgium and the Global South. The organization funds collaboration projects between researchers and teachers, and awards scholarships to students and professionals.

The VLIR-UOS program aims to strengthen higher education in the Global South and internationalization of higher education in Flanders. Through these efforts, VLIR-UOS contributes to sustainable development challenges worldwide.

The Mwecau Living Lab project is funded through VLIR-UOS as part of its mission to address global challenges through academic collaboration and knowledge exchange.
